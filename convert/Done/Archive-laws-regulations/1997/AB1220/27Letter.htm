Rulemaking Archives: AB 1220

Cover Letter: Notice of Proposed Rulemaking

July 26, 1996

To: All Assembly Bill (AB) 1220 Interested Parties

TRANSMITTAL OF CALIFORNIA INTEGRATED WASTE MANAGEMENT BOARD (CIWMB)/STATE WATER RESOURCES CONTROL BOARD (SWRCB) NOTICE OF PROPOSED RULEMAKING FOR AB 1220 PROPOSED REGULATIONS

Enclosed for your review are the Notices of Proposed Rulemaking for CIWMB and SWRCB proposed regulations implementing AB 1220 (Chapter 656, Statutes of 1993). AB 1220 requires reduction of overlap, duplication, and conflict among the CIWMB, SWRCB, and Local Enforcement Agencies. The modified regulatory language will be moved from Title 14 and Chapter 15 of Title 23 and placed in Title 27 of the California Code of Regulations (CCR). This includes all requirements for discharges of solid waste to land from Chapter 15. Requirements for discharges of hazardous waste to land will remain in Chapter 15.

The formal notice of the proposed rulemaking is on July 26, 1996. The scope of the regulations is primarily limited to AB 1220 implementation. Both the SWRCB and the CIWMB may consider making substantive changes in future rulemakings.

Please note that if you have previously requested to receive the proposed regulations, a copy is enclosed. These proposed regulations incorporate prior input from Local Enforcement Agencies, Regional Water Quality Control Boards (RWQCBs), the AB 1220 Working Group (a committee of industry, government, and environmental representatives), and interested parties.

We intend to follow a joint process for our rulemakings. This entails a joint public hearing on Wednesday, September 11 at 1:00 p.m. at the CIWMB Meeting Room, 8800 Cal Center Drive, Sacramento, CA 95826. The remainder of the process will be discussed at the hearing, but most likely will include a joint CIWMB Permitting and Enforcement Committee Meeting and SWRCB Workshop. Later, CIWMB and SWRCB will hold Board Meetings for adoption at the same time and place, if feasible. We anticipate adoption in early 1997.

The purpose of the September 11 hearing is to give the public an opportunity to make oral comments on the Proposed Regulations. Written comments must be received by the end of the public hearing in order to be considered. Each section has been clearly identified as to which board is promulgating that section.
