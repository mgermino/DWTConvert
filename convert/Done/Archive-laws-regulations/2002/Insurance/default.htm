Standards for Acceptance of Insurance as a Financial Assurance Demonstration

The Office of Administrative Law (OAL) approved the regulations and filed them with the Secretary of State on May 9, 2002. The regulations are effective on June 8, 2002.

Description
This rulemaking proposes regulatory amendments to two related financial assurance demonstration requirements under the general term of "insurance." The first clarifies that insurance demonstrations from captive insurers are considered under the same requirements and standards as insurance coverage provided by any other insurer. The second is a proposed amendment to the Board's current Certificate of Insurance for Closure, Postclosure Maintenance, and Reasonably Foreseeable Corrective Action (CIWMB 106). The second proposed amendment clarifies to the provider of insurance (insurer) that when the Board makes a claim against the insurance coverage, the insurer is liable for full payment, without delay.

Affected Regulatory Code Sections

Title 27, California Code of Regulations, Division 2, sections 22200, 22228, 22233, and 22248.

History
On June 20, 2000, the Board granted approval to begin a 45-day public comment period. Staff submitted the package to the OAL on March 19, 2001. OAL published the regulations on March 30, 2001, commencing a 45-day public comment period.

The Board held a public hearing at its regularly scheduled meeting on May 22, 2001. Following the hearing, the Board directed staff to initiate an extended 15-day comment period to allow for review of minor revisions to the regulations and additional stakeholder input.

At its August 14-15, 2001 meeting the Board directed staff to initiate a second 15-day comment period for changes made to the regulations.  The comment period ran from August 17, 2001 through September 4, 2001.

The Board adopted the regulations (August 2001 version) at its September 11-12, 2001 meeting. The Office of Administrative Law (OAL) approved the regulations and filed them with the Secretary of State on May 9, 2002. The regulations are effective on June 8, 2002.
