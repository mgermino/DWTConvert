Rulemaking Archives: Playground Safety and Recycling Act Grants

Initial Statement of Reasons

 

Chapter 7.2. Natural Resources
Article 1. General
Section 18570. Scope

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The purpose of the proposed regulation is to provide guidance to Board staff, and grant applicants, in the administration of the Grant Program established under the Playground Safety and Recycling Act (Act). The proposed regulation supports the development of markets for recycled materials suitable for use in public playgrounds. The proposed regulation cites the authority of the Board to adopt regulations for the implementation of the Program.

Technical, Theoretical, and/or Empirical Study, Report, or Document the Agency Relied Upon in Proposing the Regulation
Throughout this Initial Statement of Reasons, the following response shall be used as a response to the requirement that the above findings be made for each proposed regulatory action:

The Board did not rely upon technical, theoretical and/or empirical studies, reports, or documents for this regulatory action.

Alternatives to the Proposed Regulatory Action That Would Be as Effective and Less Burdensome to Private Persons, and Alternatives to the Proposed Regulatory Action That Would Lessen Adverse Economic Impact on Small Business.
Throughout this Initial Statement of Reasons, the following response shall be used as a response to the requirement that the above findings be made for each proposed regulatory action:

The Board considered adopting the proposed regulations as internal management procedures; however, pursuant to Health and Safety Code section 115814, the Board determined that regulations were necessary to implement the Grant Program. The Board determined that: 1) no other alternative would be as effective and less burdensome to private persons while at the same time protecting human health, safety, and the environment; and 2) no other alternative would lessen adverse economic impact on small business while protecting human health, safety and the environment.

Evidence That Supports a Finding That the Action Will Not Have a Significant Adverse Economic Impact on Business.
Throughout this Initial Statement of Reasons, the following response shall be used as a response to the requirement that the above findings be made for each proposed regulatory action:

A completed Economic and Fiscal Impact Statement, approved by the California Environmental Protection Agency and the Department of Finance, was submitted with the Emergency Rulemaking File. The completed Economic and Fiscal Impact Statement showed no private sector cost impact since the regulations set forth administrative procedures for the Board and applicants to follow for a voluntary grant program which is limited solely to local public, and local public educational, agencies.

Efforts Undertaken to Avoid Duplication or Conflict With Federal Regulations
The Grant Program is a voluntary California grant program limited to local public, and local public educational, agencies within the state and is funded by appropriations made by the state legislature. The program is intended to assist this class of California playground operators in upgrading, repairing, refurbishing, installing, or replacing public playground equipment in order to meet State safety regulations for playgrounds. The State safety regulations for playgrounds, adopted by the Department of Health Services, incorporate by reference the playground safety guidelines outlined in the Handbook for Public Playground Safety as published by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and ASTM F 1487-98, Standard Consumer Safety Performance Specification for Playground Equipment for Public Use, as published by the American Society for Testing and Materials in the Annual Book of ASTM Standards. Therefore, throughout this Initial Statement of Reasons, the following response shall be used as a response to the requirement that the above findings be made for each proposed regulatory action:

There is no known duplication or conflict of this regulation with Federal Regulations. There is no federal regulation that applies to state contracts, subcontracts, grants and loans. The proposed regulations therefore do not duplicate any federal regulations. Government Code Section 11346.2(b)(6).

Article 2. Definitions
Section 18571. Definitions of Key Terms

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The inclusion of standardized definitions of words and terms used in the proposed regulation will serve to ensure that the regulations are evenly and consistently applied to the regulated community. The purpose of this section is to define or clarify terms not sufficiently defined in the Act or existing regulations. Defining terms to be used in the administration of the Grant Program will guide the uniform application of program requirements and ensure the dissemination of consistent information for use by Board staff, grant applicants, and the public.

Article 3. Eligible Local Public Agencies and Eligible Projects
Section 18572. Eligible Local Public, and Public Educational, Agencies

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
In order to establish the eligibility of public agencies under the Act, the proposed regulation outlines two requirements of applicants. First, the applicant must operate a playground that is open to the public. Second, the applicant must demonstrate its ability to provide matching funds, either through public or private sources or from in-kind contributions. Matching funds must equal fifty percent (50%) of the requested grant amount. If the Board finds that the matching requirement would impose an extreme financial hardship (as defined), matching funds may be reduced to twenty-five percent (25%) of the requested grant amount.

The proposed regulation provides for the submission of a duly authorized joint venture application which designates the lead agency.

The proposed regulations provide notice that funding authority may limit eligible applicants to local public educational agencies.

Section 18573. Eligible Projects

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
In order for a project to be eligible under the Act, the proposed regulation sets forth three requirements. First, the project applicant must operate a public playground that has been inspected by a Certified Playground Safety Inspector prior to submission of the grant application. Second, the project must describe the need to upgrade, repair, refurbish, install, or replace the playground facility including any play equipment, surfacing, fencing, signs, internal pathways, internal landforms, vegetation, and related structures. Last, fifty percent (50%) of the grant funds awarded for the project must be expended on recycled-content equipment and/or materials.

Article 4. Criteria, Selection Process, and Required Documentation
Section 18574. Criteria

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation sets forth criteria established by the Board for the evaluation of grant applications. Since all grant applicants must provide information on the same criteria the regulation will provide a uniform tool for the Board to use in the review, scoring, and ranking of competitive applications. The criteria upon which all applicants will be evaluated and ranked include: need, objectives of the project, methodology/work statement, method to evaluate the success of the project, budget, completeness, and resource conservation.

Section 18575. Selection Process

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation provides for a method of determining which successful applicants receive funding. All applications will be ranked according to the total points received. In the event there are more qualified applicants than funding, a random number selection process may be used. All successful applicants may be placed into a common pool and a random number generator may be utilized to draw candidates from that pool. Candidates will be awarded funds in the order selected until all available funds are encumbered. Any remaining funds not awarded will be disencumbered.

Section 18575.1 Required Documentation

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation establishes a uniform method for all grant applicants to follow when submitting date-sensitive documentation to the Board. Board staff, having previously received several types of required documentation through the logistics and security at the Board’s CalEPA headquarters location, has determined that the most effective and secure delivery methods are by commercial delivery services or the U.S. Mail. The use of commercial delivery services or the U.S. Mail will ensure that all such required documentation is marked with the date that it was accepted by the carrier for delivery to the Board. The use of these services further ensures that the documentation is received in a secure central location at the CalEPA headquarters building thus reducing the likelihood of misdelivery or loss of these important materials. Any general communications which are non date-sensitive may continue to be made to the Board via telephone or fax, by letter, or by e-mail, as appropriate.

Article 5. Grant Amount and Eligible Costs
Section 18576. Apportionment of Funds

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation sets the apportionment of available funds for successful applicants. After adoption of the Emergency Regulations the Board considered administrative methods for allocating funds to successful applicants in order to ensure a fair distribution of the grant moneys among the various population groupings of the State. Taking into account the distribution of the state’s population between northern and southern California, the Board determined that a fair apportionment of available funds is based on population data provided by the Department of Finance. The Board will direct adjustments to this apportionment as updated population information becomes available.

Section 18577. Grant Amount

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation informs that the amount of funds available for each grant award are set by the law and may not exceed that amount.

Section 18578. Eligible costs

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation describes the specific activities of a grant project that are eligible for funding. These activities include those which are described in the grantee’s approved grant agreement and budget itemization, those which occur after the grantee receives a Notice to Proceed, those which occur during the grant term, and those that relate directly to the implementation of the approved grant project.

Section 18579. Ineligible costs

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation provides guidance to all parties by listing specific costs that are not eligible for reimbursement..

Section 18580. Payments

Specific Purpose and Necessity of the Regulation.
The proposed regulation places a condition on the method of payment to grantees under the program. The regulation requires that payments be made in arrears on a cost-reimbursement basis in order to ensure that payments are for work conducted on the project as approved. An additional condition is for the Board to withhold ten percent (10%) from the total of each approved grant payment amount. Any withheld and final grant amounts are payable to the grantee when all the requirements of the grant agreement have been satisfied and the final request for payment is approved.
