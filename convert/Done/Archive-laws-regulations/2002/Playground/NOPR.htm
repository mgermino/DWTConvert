Rulemaking Archives: Playground Safety and Recycling Act Grants

Notice of Proposed Rulemaking

 

Title 14 Natural Resources
Division 7 California Integrated Waste Management Board
Chapter 7.2 Playground Safety and Recycling Act Grant Program

Proposed Regulatory Action
The California Integrated Waste Management Board (Board) proposes to adopt Emergency Title 14, California Code of Regulations, Division 7, Chapter 7.2, Sections 18570 through 18580 as amended, as the final rule. The proposed final regulations establish the procedures for the Board to follow in the administration of grants awarded to local public agencies and local public educational agencies to upgrade, repair, refurbish, install, or replace public playground facilities to prevent childhood injuries on public playgrounds while developing a market for recycled materials suitable for use in public playgrounds.

Written Comment Period
Any interested person, or his or her authorized representative, may submit written comments relevant to the proposed regulatory action to the Board. The written comment period closes on August 8, 2001 and the Board will only consider comments received at the Board’s headquarters by that time. Comments may be mailed to:

Proposed Regulations
Playground Safety and Recycling Act Grant Program
1001 I Street, MS-22
P.O. Box 4025
Sacramento, CA 95812-4025

or e-mailed.

Public Hearing
A public hearing has not been scheduled. However, any interested person or his or her duly authorized representative may request, no later than 15 days prior to the close of the written comment period, a public hearing pursuant to Government Code §11346.8. Please address all requests to the Board’s contact person as designated in this Notice.

Informative Digest
The California Integrated Waste Management Act (IWM Act), Public Resources Code (PRC) Sections 40000 et seq., gives the Board authority to provide for the protection of public health, safety, and the environment through waste prevention, waste diversion, and safe waste processing and disposal. The Public Contract Code (PCC), Sections 12200 et seq., requires all local and state public agencies to purchase recycled products whenever available at no more than the total cost of nonrecycled products. It also requires vendors to specify the percentage of recycled material in the products offered. The PCC also indicates that it is the intent of the Legislature that the state pursues all feasible measures to improve markets for recycled products.

The Playground Safety and Recycling Act (H&S Code), Sections 115810 et seq., gives the Board authority to provide for the protection of the public health, safety, and the environment through administration of a grant program for local public agencies and local public educational agencies which will assist them to upgrade, repair, refurbish, install, or replace public playground facilities to prevent childhood injuries on public playgrounds while developing a market for recycled materials suitable for use in public playgrounds.

The H&S Code Section 115814 gives the Board the authority to adopt emergency regulations to develop and administer a program to provide grants to local agencies to upgrade, repair, refurbish, install, or replace public playground facilities and promote the use of recycled materials in those playground facilities.

The proposed final regulations define the scope of the Board’s authority to administer the grants program and provide clear guidelines for potential grantees entering into Board agreements. The proposed final regulations will address these issues by establishing the procedures by which the Board will administer the grants under this program.

Plain English Requirements
The proposed final regulations must satisfy the standard of clarity provided in Section 11349 of the Administrative Procedures Act. This standard requires that the regulations be written or displayed so that their meaning will be easily understood by those persons directly affected by them. The proposed final regulations are considered non-technical and were written to be easily understood by those parties which will use them. They are administrative in nature and apply to the Board’s internal management of a voluntary grant program for upgrading playgrounds to meet new safety standards and to improve markets for recycled content materials used in those playgrounds. They provide clear program guidance to Board staff and affected grant applicants.

Plain English Policy Statement
The proposed regulations provide guidance for the Board’s administration of the grants program established under the Playground Safety and Recycling Act. They provide controlling guidance to Board staff and grant applicants through the definition of key terms used in the chapter. They set forth the criteria for the eligibility of local public and local public educational agencies and for the eligibility of grant projects. They set forth the criteria established by the Board for the evaluation of applications and they set the selection process to be used. They set requirements for the submission of required documentation. The regulations also provide guidance and clarity on how grant funds will be apportioned among applicants from northern and southern California counties. The regulations also describe grant amounts, detail eligible and ineligible project costs, and describe how the Board will conduct the payment procedure.

Authority and References
H&S Code Section 115814 provides authority for these regulations. The purpose of the proposed action is to implement, and make permanent the adopted Emergency Regulations as amended.

Definitions: PRC §42002 - Postconsumer waste material, Secondary waste material. PCC §12200 - Recycled product, Postconsumer material, Secondary material. H&S Code §115735 - Playground. H&S Code §115812 - Board, Playground

Plain English: GC §11340 - Clarity of regulatory language

Playground Safety: H&S Code §115725 - Regulations for playground safety. H&S Code §115730 - Playground operators, replacement of nonconforming playgrounds.

Local Mandate and Fiscal Determinations
Board staff has determined that the proposed regulations do not impose: (1) a mandate on local public agencies or school districts; (2) significant costs or savings to any state agency; (3) costs to any local agency or school district that must be reimbursed in accordance with Government Code 17500 through 17630; (4) other nondiscretionary costs or saving on local agencies; or (5) costs or savings in federal funding to the state.

Effect on Housing Costs
Board staff has determined that the proposed regulations will not have a significant adverse impact on housing costs.

Effect on Business and Small Businesses/Small Business Determination
Board staff has determined that the proposed regulations will not apply to business and small businesses and will not have a significant adverse economic impact on business and small businesses. Any person or entity who applies for a Board agreement under these regulations must complete an application form. Only local public agencies or local public school agencies are eligible to apply under these regulations.

Effect on Competition With Out-of-State Business
Board staff has determined that the proposed regulations will not have an adverse economic impact upon the ability of California businesses to compete with out-of-state businesses.

Effect on Creation or Elimination of Jobs, Existing or New Business in the State of California
Board staff has determined that the proposed regulatory action will not affect: (1) the creation or elimination of jobs within the State of California; (2) the creation of new businesses or the elimination of existing businesses within California; or (3) the expansion of businesses currently doing business with the state.

Cost Impact on Private Persons or Enterprises
Board staff has determined that the adoption of the proposed regulations will not have a cost impact on private persons or enterprises. Any impacts of the proposed regulations already exist in current law. The proposed regulations clarify existing law and impose no new impacts.

Consideration of Alternatives
The Board must determine whether other alternatives would be more effective in carrying out the purpose for which the action is proposed or would be as effective and less burdensome to affected private persons than the proposed action.

The Board invites interested persons to present statements or arguments with respect to alternatives to the proposed regulations during the written comment period.

Availability of Statement of Reasons and Text of Proposed Regulations
The Board will have the entire rulemaking file, and all information upon which the proposed regulations are based, available for inspection and copying throughout the rulemaking process at the above address. As of the date this notice is published in the Notice Register, the rulemaking file consists of this notice, the proposed text of the regulations, and the initial statement of reasons. Copies may be obtained by contacting Jesse Adams at the address, e-mail, or phone number listed above. For more timely access to the proposed text of the regulations, and in the interest of waste prevention, interested parties are encouraged to access the Board’s Web site.

Availability of Changed or Modified Text
The Board may adopt the proposed regulations substantially as described in this notice. If the Board makes modifications which are sufficiently unrelated to the proposed text, it will make the modified text--with changes clearly indicated--available to the public for at least 15 days before the Board adopts the regulations as revised. Requests for the modified text should be made to the contact person named. The Board will mail any modified text to: all persons who testify at a public hearing if one is held; all persons who submit written comments at a public hearing; all persons whose comments are received during the comment period; and all persons who request notification of the availability of such changes. The Board will accept written comments on the modified regulations for 15 days after the date on which they are made available.
