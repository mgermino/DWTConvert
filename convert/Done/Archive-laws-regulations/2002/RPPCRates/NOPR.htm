Rulemaking Archives: Rigid Plastic Packaging Container Recycling Rates

Notice of Proposed Rulemaking

Title 14 Natural Resources
Division 7. Solid Waste
Chapter 4. Resource Conservation Programs
Article 3. Regulations for the Rigid Plastic Packaging Container Program
Section 17943. Purpose and Definitions

Proposed Regulatory Action

The California Department of Resources Recycling and Recovery (CalRecycle)proposes to amend Title 14, California Code of Regulations Division 7, Chapter 4, Article 3 by modifying section 17943. Emergency regulations were filed with the Secretary of State on September 17, 2001. The amended regulation changes the year the recycling rate is used for determining compliance with section 17944 (A) or (B) by using the actual recycling rate from the previous year to determine compliance in the current year.

Any interested person, or that person’s authorized representative, may submit written comments relevant to the proposed regulatory action to the CIWMB. Informative Digest/Plain English Policy Statement Overview

The Rigid Plastic Packaging Container (RPPC) Act of 1991 (SB 235, Hart, Chapter 769) was passed by the Legislature, was approved by the Governor on October 9, 1991, and took effect as Public Resources Code (PRC) Section 42300 et seq. on January 1, 1992. During 1993, Emergency Regulations were adopted by CIWMB, as required by PRC section 42325, and became effective on January 1, 1994.

The regulations that were in effect prior to September 17, 2001, do not explicitly state how the CIWMB must calculate its recycling rates and do not expressly mention using the previous year’s rates for compliance purposes. Further, the CIWMB’s past practice has not included the use of previous year’s rates. This practice does not give regulated product manufacturers sufficient notice in order to comply with the RPPC law by using one of the individual methods of compliance, and has resulted in a significant number of manufacturers being out of compliance and therefore subject to public hearing and possible fines.

However, the CIWMB directed staff to develop emergency regulations to amend California Code of Regulations (CCR) section 17943. The emergency regulations were filed with the Secretary of State on September 17, 2001.

The statute requires every RPPC offered for sale in California to meet, on average, one of six compliance options. These options were designed to encourage reuse and recycling of RPPCs, the use of more postconsumer resin in RPPCs and a reduction in the amount of virgin resin employed in RPPCs. RPPCs must meet one of three design criteria or be recycled at one of three specified rates. The CIWMB is required to calculate two of these rates annually; the statewide RPPC all-container rate, which is an aggregate recycling rate for all RPPCs, and the statewide recycling rate for polyethylene terephthalate (PETE) RPPCs. The overall recycling rate accounts for a variety of containers holding products such as laundry detergents, motor oil, food, cosmetics, and soft drinks. The other recycling rate includes primarily beverage and drink containers made from PETE. The statutory minimum rate for the all-container rate is 25%, and is 55% for PETE. If either of these recycling rates falls below the statutory minimum, CIWMB may require manufacturers to certify that they were individually in compliance with one of the other options during that year.

The amended regulation changes the year the recycling rate is used for determining compliance with section 17944 (A) or (B) by using the actual recycling rate from the previous year to determine compliance in the current year, which would provide advance notice to regulated product manufacturers, allowing them to take appropriate actions to ensure compliance with the RPPC law.

Impact on Small Business
The CIWMB has determined that small businesses will be not be impacted by the amended regulations.

Statement of Availability of Express Terms
The express terms of the proposed action written in plain English are available from the agency contact person named in this Notice.

Authority and References

Public Resources Code sections 40502 and 42325 provide authority for these regulations. Public Resources Code sections 42301, 42310 and 42324 are references.

Local Mandates

The CIWMB has determined that adoption of the change in the timeframe for the recycling rates does not impose a mandate on local agencies or school districts, or a mandate which requires reimbursement pursuant to Part 7 (commencing with Section 17500) of Division 4 of the Government Code.

Fiscal Impact

The CIWMB has determined that the proposed change in regulation will not result in fiscal impacts, costs or savings, to any state agency, costs or savings in federal funds to the state or other nondiscretionary costs or savings imposed on local agencies.

Housing Costs

The CIWMB has determined that the proposed change will not result in a significant impact in housing costs.

Significant Adverse Economic Impact on Business

The CIWMB has made an initial determination that the proposed change in regulation will not result in significant statewide adverse economic impact directly affecting business, including the ability of California businesses to compete with businesses in other states.

Assessment Statement

The CIWMB has assessed the potential for the proposed change to create or eliminate California jobs, create, eliminate or expand businesses within California, and has determined that there will be no effect.

Statement of Potential Cost Impact on Private Persons or Businesses Directly Affected

The CIWMB is not aware of any cost impacts that a representative private person or business would necessarily incur in reasonable compliance with the proposed action.

Consideration of Alternatives

The CIWMB must determine that no reasonable alternative considered, or that has otherwise been identified and brought to the attention of the CIWMB, would be more effective in carrying out the purpose for which the action is proposed or would be as effective and less burdensome to affected parties than the proposed action.

The CIWMB invites interested persons to present statements or arguments with respect to alternatives to the proposed amendment to the regulations at the scheduled hearing or during the written comment period.

Availability of Statement of Reasons and Text of Proposed Regulations, and Final Statement of Reasons

The CIWMB will have the entire rulemaking file, and all information upon which the proposed regulations are based, available for inspection and copying throughout the rulemaking process at the above address. As of the date this notice is published in the Notice register, the rulemaking file consists of this notice, the proposed text of the regulations, and the initial statement of reasons. The Final Statement of Reasons will also be available once it is prepared. Copies may be obtained by contacting John Nuffer at the address or phone number listed above.

Availability of Changed or Amended Text

Following a hearing, the CIWMB may adopt the proposed regulations substantially as described in this notice. If the CIWMB makes amendments which are sufficiently related to the originally proposed text, it will make the amended text--with changes clearly indicated--available to the public for at least 15 days before the CIWMB adopts the regulations as revised. Requests for the amended text should be made to the contact person named. The CIWMB will mail any amended text to: all persons who testify at the public hearing; all persons who submit written comments at the public hearing; all persons whose comments are received during the comment period; and all persons who request notification of the availability of such changes. The CIWMB will accept written comments on the amended regulations for 15 days after the date on which they are made available.
