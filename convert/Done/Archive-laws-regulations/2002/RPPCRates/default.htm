Rigid Plastic Packaging Container Recycling Rates

The amendment to regulations was approved by the Office of Administrative Law (OAL) April 10, 2002 and is effective upon filing with Secretary of State.

Description
Amendment to the California Integrated Waste Management Boardï¿½s Rigid Plastic Packaging Container (RPPC) program, which requires all RPPCs, sold or offered for sale in California to meet one of six compliance criteria. Two of the criteria are specific to the statewide RPPC all-container recycling rate and the statewide recycling rate for polyethylene terephthalate (PETE) RPPCs. The amendment will allow use of the prior year's recycling rates to determine compliance in current calendar.

Affected Regulatory Code Sections
Title 14, California Code of Regulations, Division 7, Chapter 4, Section 17943

History
The OAL approved emergency regulations and filed them with the Secretary of State on September 17, 2001. At its November 13-14, 2001 meeting, Board directed staff to begin the process to make permanent the emergency regulations and notice the regulations with OAL for the 45-day public review and comment period. A 45-day public review and comment period was noticed in the California Regulatory Notice Register on November 30, 2001.

A public hearing was held at the January 22-23, 2002 Board meeting and the permanent regulations to use the prior year recycling rates to determine compliance for the current year were approved and adopted by the Board at that meeting. The permanent regulations were filed with OAL on February 28, 2002, and  the amendment to the regulations was approved by OAL on April 10, 2002.
