Rulemaking Archives

Construction Demolition / Inert Debris

On July 10, 2003, the Office of Administrative Law approved the Phase I regulations (with some modifications) and filed them with the Secretary of State. The regulations were effective on August 9, 2003.

On December 26, 2003, the Office of Administrative Law approved the Phase II regulations and filed them with the Secretary of State. The regulations are effective as of February 24, 2004.

Description
Placement of facilities and operations handling construction and demolition (C&D) and/or inert debris into regulatory tiers, and setting minimum standards.

Affected Regulatory Code Sections
Title 14, California Code of Regulations, Division 7, Chapter 3

Article 4, Section 17225.15,
Article 5.9, Sections 17380 through 17386, and
Article 5.95, Sections 17387 through 17390

Title 14, California Code of Regulations, Division 7, Chapter 5

Article 3.2 Sections 18223, 18223.5, 18223.6

Title 27, California Code of Regulations, Division 2, Chapter 4

Article 1, Section 21565 and
Article 2, Section 21570

Implementation Guidance

Rulemaking History

Following the close of a 45-day comment period on December 28, 1998, the Board held a public hearing in Sacramento on January 13, 1999. At its January 27-28, 1999 meeting, the Board directed staff to collect additional information on mine reclamation activities within the state and return to the Board to request approval for an additional 15-day comment period. At its August 31, 1999 meeting in Sacramento, the Board approved a revised draft of the proposed regulations for an additional 15-day comment period. At its September 22, 1999 meeting, the Board voted 4-0 to not adopt the proposed C&D/Inert Debris regulations and to have further discussions with the State Water Resources Control Board, the Department of Conservation, and other stakeholders before developing a new draft.

In December 2000, the Board approved a two-phase approach to reestablishing this rulemaking. Phase I covers the transfer and processing of construction, demolition, and inert debris. Phase II covers the disposal of construction, demolition, and inert debris.

At the August 14-15, 2001, meeting staff presented to the Board an agenda item containing a proposed schedule with major milestones, as well as a copy of the initial draft proposed regulations for Phase I. 

Board staff conducted public workshops on September 4, 2001, in Diamond Bar and on September 19, 2001, in Sacramento to solicit feedback on the draft regulations for Phase I. At its November 13-14, 2001 meeting the Board considered approving proposed Phase I regulations for a 45-day comment period.  The Board directed staff to consult with stakeholders on issues associated with the current draft and return with a revised draft. At its December 11-12, 2001 meeting, the Board directed staff to further consult with stakeholders on issues associated with the current draft of the proposed Phase I regulations and bring another draft version of the regulations back to the January 22-23, 2002, Board meeting for consideration of approval to begin a 45-day comment period.

Board staff conducted a public workshops on December 13, 2001 in Diamond Bar and in Sacramento on January 10, 2002, to solicit input on both Phase I and Phase II.

Board staff conducted public workshops on March 4, 2002, in Sacramento and on March 5, 2002, in Diamond Bar to solicit input on Phase II draft regulations.

At its January 22-23, 2002, meeting the Board directed staff to begin the formal rulemaking process for Phase I by publishing a notice for a 45-day comment period. A 45-day comment period for Phase I ran from May 31, 2002 through July 15, 2002. Staff conducted public workshops on July 22 in Sacramento and July 31 in Diamond Bar to solicit comment on the proposed Phase I regulations. The Board held a public hearing for Phase I on August 12, 2002. At its September 17, 2002 meeting the Board directed staff to make specified changes to the Phase I regulation text and return to the November 12, 2002 Permitting and Enforcement Committee meeting with a request for direction to initiate an additional comment period on the changes. At its November 12, 2002 meeting the Permitting and Enforcement Committee directed staff to make specified changes to the Phase I regulation text and return to the December 2, 2002 Permitting and Enforcement Committee meeting with a request for direction to initiate an additional comment period on the changes. The Committee directed staff to make further changes and bring the revised regulation text to the December 10-11, 2002 Board meeting with a request for direction to initiate an additional comment period on the changes. At its December 10, 2002 meeting the Board directed staff to make specified changes to the Phase I regulation text and initiate a 15-day comment period on the changes. The comment period ran from December 20, 2002 through January 3, 2003. At the January 14, 2003 Board meeting staff was directed to make specified changes to the Phase I regulation text and initiate a second 15-day comment period on the changes. The comment period ran from January 24 through February 10, 2003. At the March 18, 2003 Board meeting staff was directed to make specified changes to the Phase I regulation text and initiate a third 15-day comment period on the changes. The comment period ran from March 22 through April 7, 2003.

At its April 8, 2002 meeting the Permitting and Enforcement Committee directed staff to begin a 45-day comment period for the Phase II regulations. The comment period ran from January 17 through March 3, 2003. The Board held a public hearing at the April 7, 2003 Permitting and Enforcement Committee meeting.

At its April 9, 2003 meeting the Board adopted Phase I regulation text. On July 10 the Office of Administrative Law approved the Phase I regulations, which are effective on August 9, 2003.

At its July 7, 2003 meeting the Permitting and Enforcement Committee directed staff to begin an additional 15-day comment period for changes to the Phase II regulations. The comment period ran from July 9 through July 24, 2003. At its August 4, 2003 meeting the Permitting and Enforcement Committee directed staff to begin a second 15-day comment period for changes to the Phase II regulations. The comment period ran from August 13 through August 28, 2003.

On July 10, 2003, the Office of Administrative Law approved the Phase I regulations (with some modifications) and filed them with the Secretary of State. The regulations were effective on August 9, 2003.

At its September 17, 2003 meeting the Board adopted Phase II regulations with the following proposed text alternatives (see proposed text below):

  • Issue 1--Alternative 3
  • Issue 2--Alternative 3
  • Issue 3--Alternative 2
  • Issue 4--Alternative 2

On November 10, 2003, staff submitted the Phase II final rulemaking file to the Office of Administrative Law for a 30-working-day review.

On December 26, 2003, the Office of Administrative Law approved the Phase II regulations and filed them with the Secretary of State.
