Tire Monofills

 
The Office of Administrative Law approved the regulations on October 14, 2003 and they are effective on November 13, 2003.

Description
Regulations for permitting of tire monofills under the solid waste regulations, as well as standards for monofilling within solid waste facilities.

Affected Regulatory Code Sections
Title 14, California Code of Regulations, Division 7, Chapter 3 Article 5.4, Sections 17346 through 17349

Rulemaking History
Board staff held public workshops in October 1999 to receive informal public comment on the proposed Tire Facility, Haulers, and Monofill regulations. At its February 2000 meeting the Board directed staff to separate proposed tire monofill regulations from the Tire Facility, Haulers, and Monofill package, and to conduct workshops on waste tire monofilling prior to reporting back to the Board in June 2000. 

Board staff held a second workshop on June 12, 2000, to discuss, among other topics, the permitting of tire monofills under the solid waste permitting regulations. The consensus at the meeting was that the permitting of tire monofills should be part of the solid waste disposal facility regulations, but that the requirements for tire monofill cells at existing solid waste disposal facilities should be handled under a later regulation package in order not to impede the progress of the tire monofill regulations. At the June 20-21, 2000 Board meeting, the Board directed staff to separate the monofill regulations into two phases and to give first priority to the permitting of tire monofills under the solid waste regulations. The setting of standards for codisposal within solid waste facilities will follow.

Staff distributed draft tire monofill regulations for comment on March 7, 2001. Board staff held a third workshop on March 12, 2001 to discuss the draft regulations. On April 12, 2001 the Board staff held a fourth workshop.

At its May 22-23, 2001 meeting the Board approved the regulations, with minor changes, for a 45-day public comment period. The comment period ran from September 6 through October 21, 2002. The Board held a public hearing at the November 12, 2002 Permitting and Enforcement Committee meeting.

At its January 6, 2003 meeting the Permitting and Enforcement Committee directed staff to begin a 15-day public comment period on changes to the proposed text. The comment period ran from January 17 through February 3, 2003.

At its March 10, 2003 meeting the Permitting and Enforcement Committee directed staff to begin a second 15-day public comment period on changes to the proposed text. The comment period ran from March 31 through April 14, 2003.

The Board adopted these proposed regulations at its May 13, 2003 meeting. The Office of Administrative Law approved the regulations on October 14, 2003 and they are effective on November 13, 2003.
